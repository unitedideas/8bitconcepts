First comment:

```text
The useful shift here is not "cloud AI is dead." It is hierarchy.

Cloud models still handle frontier work. Device-side models handle the everyday intelligence layer: fast local actions, private context, offline tolerance, and workflows where sending every prompt to a hosted endpoint is unnecessary or expensive.

The paper lays out the main signals I am watching: Apple/Qualcomm/Intel NPUs becoming default hardware, small models getting good enough for routine tasks, cloud inference becoming scarce infrastructure, and memory/context moving closer to the user.

The practical design question is which parts of an AI workflow should be owned intelligence versus managed intelligence.
```
